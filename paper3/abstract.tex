High-stakes AI systems operating under the EU AI Act and LGPD face a
fundamental conflict: accountability law mandates an append-only audit trail
of every decision, while privacy law (GDPR Art.~17, LGPD Art.~18) grants
data subjects the right to erasure. Prior work on redactable authenticated
data structures resolves the individual right-to-erasure problem but
introduces a new attack surface: a system operator can satisfy GDPR
compliance requests selectively, erasing only decisions that constitute
evidence of algorithmic discrimination while retaining favorable ones. We
call this the \emph{Selective Deletion Attack} and show that no existing
redactable blockchain scheme detects it, because all prior work models the
operator as a trusted party and the data subject as the sole source of
erasure requests.

This paper presents \textbf{Non-Discriminatory Redaction (NDR)}, a
cryptographic protocol for AI decision transparency logs that makes the
Selective Deletion Attack verifiably detectable. NDR augments a standard
append-only log (as in BTV-Transparency~\cite{btv-t-repo}) with three
primitives: (1)~a \emph{RedactionReceipt} that appends the erasure event
itself to the log, making redactions auditable under the same append-only
guarantee; (2)~a chameleon-hash-based leaf commitment that makes individual
decision content irrecoverable after redaction while preserving Merkle-root
continuity; and (3)~a \emph{StatConsistencyProof} --- a ZK-SNARK proving
that the conditional approval rates of all protected demographic groups
(as defined by the operator's fairness policy) shift by at most~$\varepsilon$
after a batch of redactions.

We define \emph{$\varepsilon$-statistical consistency} formally, give a
construction in Noir over a Pedersen-committed demographic attribute scheme,
and prove that any redaction batch accepted by the verifier satisfies
both soundness (the Merkle root reflects the post-redaction state faithfully)
and indistinguishability (redacted leaves are computationally
indistinguishable from blank commitments). Empirical evaluation shows proof
generation costs of approximately 2.1~s per batch of 1{,}000 decisions on a
commodity server, with constant-size proofs (\textasciitilde3.2~kB).
NDR composes with Papers~1 and~2 of this series to close the full
accountability loop: no decision without evidence, no delivery without
persistence, and no redaction without statistical justification.
